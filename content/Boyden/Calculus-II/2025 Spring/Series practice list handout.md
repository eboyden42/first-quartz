%% Worksheet 11.7b %%
Determine whether each of the series is absolutely convergent, conditionally convergent, or divergent, using any convergence test: 

(1) $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^\infty\left(1+\frac{1}{n}\right)2^{-n}$ $\qquad$ (2) $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^\infty\sin\left(\left(\frac{2n+1}{2}\right)\pi\right)n^{-1/2}$ $\qquad$ (3) $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^\infty\frac{\ln n}{n+2}$

(4) $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^\infty(-1)^n\ln\left(\frac{n}{3n+1}\right)$ $\qquad$ (5) $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^\infty\frac{(-2)^n}{n^2}$ $\qquad$ (6) $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^\infty\left(\frac{1}{9}\right)^n(2n)!$

(7) $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^\infty\frac{1\cdot3\cdot5\cdots(2n-1)}{5^n\, n!}$ $\qquad$ (8) $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^\infty\frac{(-1)^n}{3n}$ $\qquad$ (9) $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^\infty\left(\frac{-1}{3n}\right)^n$

(10) $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^\infty(-1)^n\frac{n^{2n}}{\left(1+2n^2\right)^n}$ $\qquad$ (11) $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^\infty(-1)^n\frac{n}{n^4+1}$ $\qquad$ (12) $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^\infty(-1)^n\frac{\ln n}{\sqrt{n}}$

(13) $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^\infty\frac{\ln n}{n^2}$
